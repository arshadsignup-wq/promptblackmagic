Landing Page Conversion Architect

Describe your product and audience to get a complete high-converting landing page structure with copy, layout, and CTA strategy.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Paste into any LLM with your product details. Get back a full landing page blueprint you can hand to a designer or build yourself.

The Prompt

You are a conversion rate optimization expert who has designed landing pages that generated over $100M in revenue. You combine direct-response copywriting with modern UX psychology.

Product/Service: [WHAT YOU'RE SELLING]
Target audience: [WHO THEY ARE AND THEIR BIGGEST PAIN POINT]
Price point: [PRICE OR PRICING MODEL]
Goal: [SIGN UP/BUY/BOOK A CALL/DOWNLOAD]
Current conversion rate: [IF KNOWN, OR 'NEW PAGE']

Design a complete landing page blueprint:

**1. Above the Fold (Hero Section)**
- Headline formula (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
- Write 3 headline variations to A/B test
- Subheadline that adds specificity
- Primary CTA button (exact text, color psychology reasoning)
- Hero image/video direction
- Social proof element (what type and where)

**2. Problem Section**
- 3 pain points that make visitors feel understood
- Agitation copy that raises the stakes
- Emotional trigger words specific to this audience

**3. Solution Section**
- How your product solves each pain point
- Feature-to-benefit translation table
- Visual layout recommendation (icons, screenshots, demo)

**4. Social Proof Stack**
- Testimonial placement strategy (which types, how many)
- Trust badge recommendations
- Numbers/stats to display
- Logo bar if B2B

**5. Objection Handling**
- Top 5 objections this audience will have
- FAQ copy that neutralizes each
- Risk reversal (guarantee copy)

**6. Final CTA Section**
- Urgency/scarcity element (ethical, not fake)
- Final push copy
- Secondary CTA for not-ready visitors

**7. Technical Specs**
- Page load optimization priorities
- Mobile-first layout rules
- Tracking setup (what to measure)

Write all copy in the brand's voice. Make it specific, not generic.

Pro Tips for UI/UX Design

  • Request specific component specifications (spacing, typography scale, color tokens) rather than vague layout descriptions.
  • Include your current conversion rate or usability issue so the AI can target specific improvements rather than generic best practices.
  • Include your target user persona and their primary task when prompting for design recommendations - context drives better UX advice.

When to Use This Prompt

  • Your checkout flow has a high abandonment rate and you need specific fixes to reduce friction.
  • You are launching a new product and need a landing page that converts visitors into customers or leads.
  • Your mobile app has high download rates but poor retention, and you suspect the onboarding flow is the problem.

Expected Results

  • Design system tokens including typography scales, spacing systems, and color semantics.
  • Detailed page layouts with section-by-section wireframe descriptions, component specs, and content hierarchy.
  • Conversion-optimized copy for headlines, CTAs, error messages, and micro-copy throughout the interface.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Swap the target audience with your specific user persona including their technical sophistication level.
  • Change the platform specification to match your tech stack (React, Shopify, WordPress, custom).
  • Adjust the design style references to match your existing brand guidelines and visual identity.
