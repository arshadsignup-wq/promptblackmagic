Systematic Literature Review Guide

Conduct a rigorous systematic literature review with PRISMA methodology, search strategies, and synthesis frameworks.

By Arshad Hossain

How to Use

Copy & paste the prompt below into your preferred LLM. Unless a specific AI model is mentioned, you can use whichever you prefer.

The Prompt

You are a research methodology expert. Guide me through a systematic literature review.

Review parameters:
- Research topic: [YOUR TOPIC]
- Research question: [YOUR QUESTION, OR "HELP ME FORMULATE ONE"]
- Discipline: [YOUR FIELD]
- Review type: [SYSTEMATIC / META-ANALYSIS / SCOPING / RAPID]
- Purpose: [DISSERTATION / JOURNAL / GRANT / THESIS]
- Timeline: [HOW LONG DO YOU HAVE]
- Software available: [COVIDENCE / RAYYAN / ENDNOTE / ZOTERO / EXCEL]

Deliver:
1. **Research Question:** PICO/SPIDER framework, scope boundaries, primary and sub-questions
2. **Protocol:** PROSPERO registration guidance, PRISMA-P outline, amendment policy
3. **Search Strategy:** Database selection with rationale, complete search strings with Boolean operators and MeSH terms, grey literature sources, hand-searching strategy
4. **Eligibility Criteria:** Inclusion/exclusion with justification, decision rules for borderline cases, 5 sample screening exercises
5. **Screening Process:** Title/abstract and full-text protocols, inter-rater reliability, PRISMA flow diagram template
6. **Data Extraction:** Complete extraction form template (study ID, design, population, intervention, outcomes, quality)
7. **Quality Assessment:** Recommended appraisal tool for your study types (RoB 2, Newcastle-Ottawa, CASP, MMAT)
8. **Synthesis:** Narrative synthesis framework or meta-analysis considerations (effect sizes, heterogeneity, forest plots)
9. **Reporting:** PRISMA checklist walkthrough
10. **Common Mistakes:** Top 10 errors that lead to rejection at peer review

Enable a publishable systematic review from start to finish.

Why "Systematic Literature Review Guide" Works

"Systematic Literature Review Guide" delivers consistent results because it tells the AI exactly what role to play, what context matters, and what format the output should take. You can expect methodologically sound research outputs with clear limitations and actionable recommendations - the kind of result that normally requires several rounds of prompt refinement.

  • Define your research question clearly and specify the scope (academic, market research, competitive analysis) for focused, useful results.
  • Include your existing knowledge and hypotheses so the AI can build on what you know rather than starting from basics.

When to Use "Systematic Literature Review Guide"

"Systematic Literature Review Guide" is particularly useful in these situations. If any of these scenarios sound familiar, this prompt will save you significant time.

  • Your analysis is complete but you need to structure findings into a report format that decision-makers will actually read.
  • You want to identify trends in your industry and need a research methodology that separates signal from noise.
  • Your research question is too broad and you need help narrowing it into a testable hypothesis with clear variables.

What You Will Get from "Systematic Literature Review Guide"

When you use "Systematic Literature Review Guide" with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, here is what to expect in the AI output.

  • Trend analysis reports that separate verified patterns from noise using defined statistical thresholds.
  • Research methodology frameworks that define data collection, sample selection, and analysis approaches.
  • Executive research briefings that distill complex findings into actionable recommendations.

How to Customize "Systematic Literature Review Guide"

Adapt "Systematic Literature Review Guide" to your specific situation by modifying these key areas. The more context you add, the better the results.

  • Swap the sample variables with your actual factors of interest so the methodology addresses your real question.
  • Add your research purpose (academic paper, business decision, competitive analysis) to set the right depth and rigor.
  • Include your preferred sources (academic journals, industry reports, government data) for targeted source recommendations.
