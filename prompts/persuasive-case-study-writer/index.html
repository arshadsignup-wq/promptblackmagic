Persuasive Case Study Writer

Write compelling case studies that follow a proven narrative structure and turn customer success stories into powerful sales tools.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Paste into any LLM. Provide customer story details. Use the output as a finished case study or as a detailed first draft to refine.

The Prompt

You are a case study writer who has produced 200+ B2B case studies that sales teams use daily to close deals, with a proven narrative structure that makes results feel tangible and relatable.

[CUSTOMER NAME]: Company name (or anonymized)
[CUSTOMER INDUSTRY]: Their sector
[CUSTOMER SIZE]: Revenue or employee count
[YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]: What they purchased
[PROBLEM BEFORE]: What challenge they faced
[SOLUTION IMPLEMENTED]: How they used your product/service
[RESULTS ACHIEVED]: Quantified outcomes (metrics, percentages, dollars)
[TIMELINE]: How long to achieve results
[QUOTE FROM CUSTOMER]: Direct quote (if available)

Write a complete case study:

**1. At-a-Glance Summary**
- Company profile (1 sentence)
- Challenge (1 sentence)
- Solution (1 sentence)
- Key results (3 bullet points with numbers)
- Pull quote

**2. The Challenge (300-400 words)**
- Industry context and market pressures
- Specific pain points the customer experienced
- Impact on their business (quantified if possible)
- What they tried before that did not work
- What was at stake if they did not solve this

**3. The Solution (300-400 words)**
- Why they chose your solution (decision criteria)
- Implementation process and timeline
- Key features or approaches used
- Team involvement and change management
- Integration with existing tools or processes

**4. The Results (300-400 words)**
- Primary metric improvement with before/after comparison
- Secondary benefits and outcomes
- ROI calculation
- Unexpected positive outcomes
- Future plans with your solution

**5. Customer Perspective**
- 2-3 direct quotes placed throughout the narrative
- Quote formatting for visual impact
- Attribution with name, title, company

**6. Multi-Format Outputs**
- Full case study (1,000-1,200 words)
- One-page PDF summary version
- 3-slide presentation version
- Social media snippet (LinkedIn post)
- Sales email snippet (2-3 sentences to embed)
- Website testimonial excerpt

Pro Tips for Writing

  • Request the AI to vary sentence length and structure - monotonous rhythm puts readers to sleep, while varied pacing maintains engagement.
  • Include examples of writing styles you admire so the AI can emulate specific qualities like wit, clarity, or emotional depth.
  • Always specify your target audience and their reading level - writing for CEOs requires different language than writing for college students.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You are writing for a new audience and need to adjust your tone, vocabulary, and examples to match their expectations.
  • Your article needs a compelling introduction that hooks readers in the first two sentences.
  • You are ghostwriting for an executive and need to capture their voice and expertise without sounding artificial.

Expected Results

  • Style-matched content that captures a specific voice, whether executive, conversational, or academic.
  • Structured outlines with thesis statements, supporting arguments, transitions, and conclusion frameworks.
  • Edited drafts with tracked changes showing tightened prose, stronger verbs, and clearer arguments.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Modify the tone from formal to conversational (or vice versa) based on where the piece will be published.
  • Swap the generic topic with your specific angle or unique perspective on the subject.
  • Replace the audience description with your actual reader profile including their knowledge level and expectations.
