Product Launch Email Campaign

Create a complete email sequence for launching a product or feature, from teaser to announcement to follow-up.

By Arshad Hossain

How to Use

Copy & paste the prompt below into your preferred LLM. Unless a specific AI model is mentioned, you can use whichever you prefer.

The Prompt

You are a product launch email strategist. Build a launch email campaign that maximizes day-one sales and sustained momentum.

Launch details:
- Product: [WHAT ARE YOU LAUNCHING?]
- Price: [PRICE POINT]
- Launch date: [DATE]
- Audience size: [EMAIL LIST SIZE]
- Pre-launch runway: [HOW MANY DAYS/WEEKS BEFORE LAUNCH?]
- Special offer: [EARLY BIRD DISCOUNT / BONUS / LIMITED QUANTITY / NONE]
- Target: [SALES NUMBER / SIGNUPS / DOWNLOADS]

Build a complete launch email sequence:

**Pre-Launch Phase (1-2 weeks before):**
1. The Teaser: Build curiosity without revealing everything
2. The Problem: Agitate the pain point this product solves
3. The Social Proof: Share beta results, testimonials, or behind-the-scenes

**Launch Phase (launch day + 2 days):**
4. The Announcement: "It's here" — clear, exciting, with all details and primary CTA
5. The Deep Dive: Feature breakdown, use cases, FAQ
6. The Urgency: Early bird deadline, limited availability, or bonus expiration

**Post-Launch Phase (3-7 days after):**
7. The Case Study: Customer success story or detailed walkthrough
8. The Final Call: Last chance to get launch pricing/bonuses

For each email: subject line (3 options), full copy, CTA, and timing.

Also provide:
- **Non-Opener Strategy:** Re-send to non-openers with different subject lines
- **Segmentation:** Different messaging for warm leads vs. cold subscribers
- **SMS Companion Messages:** If applicable, 3-4 SMS messages to complement the email sequence

Why "Product Launch Email Campaign" Works

The reason "Product Launch Email Campaign" outperforms a generic request is structural: it uses sequential task breakdown and audience specification and success criteria to constrain the AI's response toward high-converting email copy with subject lines, body text, and CTAs aligned to your campaign goal. This means you get high-converting email copy with subject lines, body text, and CTAs aligned to your campaign goal without the trial-and-error that wastes most people's time with AI.

Pro Tips for Using "Product Launch Email Campaign"

These email marketing tips will help you get stronger results when using "Product Launch Email Campaign" and similar prompts in this category.

  • Include your brand voice guidelines in the prompt - casual vs. professional tone makes a huge difference in email engagement.
  • Request mobile-friendly formatting - over 60% of emails are read on phones, so short paragraphs and clear CTAs are essential.
  • Always specify the type of email (welcome series, promotional, re-engagement, nurture) so the AI matches tone and structure to the purpose.

When to Use "Product Launch Email Campaign"

"Product Launch Email Campaign" is particularly useful in these situations. If any of these scenarios sound familiar, this prompt will save you significant time.

  • Your nurture sequence needs to educate prospects over six weeks before they are ready to buy your high-ticket offer.
  • You are launching a welcome email sequence and need messages that convert new subscribers into first-time buyers.
  • You are building an abandoned cart sequence and need escalating urgency across three to four follow-up emails.

What You Will Get from "Product Launch Email Campaign"

When you use "Product Launch Email Campaign" with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, here is what to expect in the AI output.

  • Segmentation strategies that match email content to subscriber behavior and lifecycle stage.
  • Re-engagement sequences with escalating incentives designed to win back inactive subscribers.
  • Complete email sequences with subject lines, preview text, body copy, and calls to action for each message.

How to Customize "Product Launch Email Campaign"

Adapt "Product Launch Email Campaign" to your specific situation by modifying these key areas. The more context you add, the better the results.

  • Replace the brand name placeholder with your actual company name and include your tagline for consistency.
  • Add your specific offer details including pricing, discount percentage, and expiration date.
  • Modify the CTA to reflect your actual desired action: purchase, book a call, download, or sign up.
