Complete Brand Identity Brief

Get a comprehensive brand identity brief covering name exploration, visual direction, typography, color psychology, and design system specs.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Paste into any LLM. Use the output as a brief for your designer or branding agency.

The Prompt

You are a creative director at a boutique branding agency. You've built brand identities from scratch for over 200 companies.

Business: [WHAT THE BUSINESS DOES]
Target market: [WHO YOU SERVE]
Brand personality: [3-5 ADJECTIVE DESCRIPTORS]
Competitor brands to differentiate from: [2-3 NAMES]
Any existing brand elements to keep: [LOGO, COLORS, OR 'STARTING FRESH']
Budget context: [STARTUP/SMB/ENTERPRISE]

Create a complete brand identity brief:

**1. Brand Name Exploration (if needed)**
- 10 name concepts across different naming strategies:
  - Descriptive (says what you do)
  - Abstract (unique invented word)
  - Metaphorical (evocative of a quality)
  - Founder/personal
  - Acronym
- Domain availability check approach
- Trademark screening notes

**2. Visual Identity Direction**
- 3 distinct mood board descriptions (different visual directions)
  - Direction A: [describe aesthetic, references, feeling]
  - Direction B: [describe aesthetic, references, feeling]
  - Direction C: [describe aesthetic, references, feeling]
- Recommend one direction with rationale

**3. Color System**
- Primary color with hex, RGB, CMYK (with color psychology justification)
- Secondary color palette (3-4 colors with codes)
- Neutral palette (backgrounds, text)
- Accent/action color
- Color usage ratios (60-30-10 rule application)
- Accessibility contrast check guidance

**4. Typography System**
- Heading font recommendation (with Google Fonts or Adobe alternatives)
- Body text font recommendation
- Accent/display font if needed
- Type scale (sizes for H1 through body text)
- Font pairing rationale

**5. Logo Direction**
- Logo concept descriptions (3 directions)
- Logo usage rules (minimum size, clear space, backgrounds)
- Logo variations needed (full, icon, monochrome, reversed)
- Favicon and app icon specifications

**6. Brand Applications Preview**
- Business card layout description
- Email signature format
- Social media profile/cover image direction
- Presentation template direction
- Website header and hero treatment

**7. Brand Guidelines Table of Contents**
- Outline what the final brand guidelines document should contain
- File deliverables checklist

Make this specific enough to hand to a designer as a working brief.

Pro Tips for Branding & Identity

  • Specify your target audience demographics and psychographics - brand voice should resonate with who you are trying to reach.
  • Ask for specific hex color codes, font names, and spacing values rather than vague visual direction.
  • Include your brand values and personality adjectives as constraints so every recommendation stays on-brand.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You are onboarding new team members or freelance writers who need to learn your brand voice quickly.
  • You are launching a new business and need to define your brand voice, visual identity, and positioning from scratch.
  • Your brand messaging feels inconsistent across channels and you need a unified voice guide for your team.

Expected Results

  • Visual identity specifications with color codes, typography pairings, and design system foundations.
  • Before-and-after copy rewrites demonstrating generic content transformed into on-brand messaging.
  • Quick-reference brand cards that any team member can use to write consistently on-brand.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Swap the personality adjectives with words that genuinely describe how you want your brand to feel.
  • Adjust the competitor references to your real competitors so differentiation advice is actionable.
  • Include your existing brand elements (colors, fonts, logo) if you are evolving rather than starting fresh.
