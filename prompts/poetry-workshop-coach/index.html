Poetry Workshop Coach

Get guided poetry writing with form explanations, feedback on drafts, and techniques for rhythm, imagery, and voice.

By Arshad Hossain

How to Use

Copy & paste the prompt below into your preferred LLM. Unless a specific AI model is mentioned, you can use whichever you prefer.

The Prompt

You are a poetry professor and published poet. Guide me through writing a poem.

My poem:
- Topic/Theme: [WHAT IS THE POEM ABOUT?]
- Form: [FREE VERSE / SONNET / HAIKU / VILLANELLE / LIMERICK / SPOKEN WORD / "HELP ME CHOOSE"]
- Tone: [CONTEMPLATIVE / PASSIONATE / HUMOROUS / DARK / TENDER / ANGRY]
- Draft (if you have one): [PASTE YOUR DRAFT, OR "STARTING FROM SCRATCH"]
- Experience level: [FIRST POEM / OCCASIONAL WRITER / EXPERIENCED POET]
- Poets I admire: [LIST POETS OR "NO PREFERENCE"]

Deliver:
1. **If starting from scratch:**
   - 5 opening line options that immediately create tension or beauty
   - A structure recommendation based on your theme and experience level
   - Guided prompts: sensory details (what does this theme look, sound, feel, smell, taste like?)
   - A complete draft with annotations explaining craft choices

2. **If editing a draft:**
   - Line-by-line analysis: what works, what doesn't, and why
   - Identify the poem's strongest moment and build toward it
   - Replace abstract language with concrete imagery
   - Improve sound quality: alliteration, assonance, consonance opportunities
   - Cut unnecessary words (poetry is about precision)
   - Suggest line break alternatives that change meaning or emphasis
   - Provide an edited version alongside the original

3. **Craft Lessons:** Based on your poem, teach 3 specific techniques with examples
4. **Reading Recommendations:** 3 poems by other poets that explore similar themes brilliantly
5. **Workshop Questions:** 5 questions a workshop group would ask about this poem

Poetry isn't about being clever. It's about seeing the world so clearly you make others see it too.

Why "Poetry Workshop Coach" Works

What separates "Poetry Workshop Coach" from an off-the-cuff AI question is precision. It applies tone calibration and success criteria and depth requirements, which gives the model enough direction to produce original creative directions with enough specificity to execute without additional briefing. Expect original creative directions with enough specificity to execute without additional briefing. The constraints in this prompt prevent the model from falling back on vague, unhelpful responses.

Pro Tips for Using "Poetry Workshop Coach"

These creative tips will help you get stronger results when using "Poetry Workshop Coach" and similar prompts in this category.

  • Include emotional tone and sensory details in your prompts. "Write something sad" is weak; "write something that captures the quiet ache of watching someone leave" is strong.
  • Ask for unexpected combinations and novel angles. Prompt the AI to "surprise you" or "combine two unrelated concepts" for truly original ideas.
  • Request multiple creative options (5+) and then ask the AI to combine the best elements from each into a final version.

When to Use "Poetry Workshop Coach"

"Poetry Workshop Coach" is particularly useful in these situations. If any of these scenarios sound familiar, this prompt will save you significant time.

  • You are brainstorming concepts for a brand campaign and need 20 original ideas you can narrow down to the top three.
  • You are writing a short story and need help developing characters with believable motivations and distinct voices.
  • You want to remix an existing creative format in an unexpected way that feels fresh without losing the original appeal.

What You Will Get from "Poetry Workshop Coach"

When you use "Poetry Workshop Coach" with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, here is what to expect in the AI output.

  • Character profiles with backstories, motivations, speech patterns, and relationship dynamics.
  • Content series frameworks with unifying themes, episode arcs, and audience progression strategies.
  • Multiple creative concepts with unique angles, themes, and execution approaches for client selection.

How to Customize "Poetry Workshop Coach"

Adapt "Poetry Workshop Coach" to your specific situation by modifying these key areas. The more context you add, the better the results.

  • Adjust the creative constraints (word limit, format, required elements) to match your actual project brief.
  • Modify the emotional tone to match the specific feeling you want the audience to experience.
  • Replace the genre placeholder with your specific creative medium: brand campaign, short fiction, video script, or visual concept.
