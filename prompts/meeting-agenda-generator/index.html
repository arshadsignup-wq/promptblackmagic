Meeting Agenda Generator

Create structured meeting agendas with time allocations, discussion topics, pre-read materials, and action item templates for any meeting type.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Fill in your meeting details and paste into any LLM. Share the generated agenda with attendees before the meeting.

The Prompt

You are a meeting effectiveness specialist. Design an agenda that makes this meeting worth everyone's time.

Meeting type: [TEAM STANDUP / PROJECT KICKOFF / QUARTERLY REVIEW / 1-ON-1 / BRAINSTORM / CLIENT CALL / BOARD MEETING]
Duration: [15 / 30 / 60 / 90 MINUTES]
Attendees: [NUMBER AND ROLES]
Goal: [WHAT MUST BE DECIDED OR ACCOMPLISHED BY THE END]

Create a meeting agenda with:

1. **Meeting header:** Title, date, time, attendees, meeting goal (one sentence)

2. **Pre-meeting preparation:**
   - Documents to review before the meeting (with links/locations)
   - Questions attendees should come prepared to answer
   - Data or updates each person should bring

3. **Timed agenda:**
   - Each item with: topic, owner, time allocation, purpose (inform / discuss / decide)
   - First 10% for alignment (review goals, context)
   - Middle 70% for substance (discussion, decisions)
   - Last 20% for action items and next steps

4. **Discussion framework:** For each agenda item, include:
   - The question to answer or decision to make
   - Relevant context in 1-2 sentences
   - Options to consider (if decision-making)

5. **Action item template:**
   | Action | Owner | Deadline | Status |
   |--------|-------|----------|--------|

6. **Meeting rules:** 3 ground rules for an effective meeting (e.g., cameras on, no multitasking, decisions require quorum)

Keep the agenda tight. If a topic does not require this group, cut it. Every minute should justify pulling these people from their work.

Pro Tips for Productivity

  • Ask the AI to create time-blocked schedules rather than simple task lists. Allocating specific hours forces realistic planning.
  • Include your tools and apps (Notion, Todoist, Google Calendar) so the AI recommends workflows that fit your existing setup.
  • Request the AI to apply the 80/20 principle - identify which 20% of tasks drive 80% of your results and prioritize those.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You are managing multiple projects across teams and need a system that prevents anything from falling through the cracks.
  • Your meetings are eating your calendar and you need frameworks to cut meeting time in half without losing alignment.
  • You are onboarding onto a new role and need a 30-60-90 day plan that shows your manager you are ramping quickly.

Expected Results

  • Time-blocked daily schedules that protect deep work hours and batch similar tasks together.
  • Weekly review templates with reflection prompts, metric tracking, and next-week planning sections.
  • Meeting agenda formats that cut discussion time while improving decision quality and action item clarity.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Add your specific productivity tools (Notion, Asana, Todoist) so workflows integrate with your existing stack.
  • Include your top three current priorities so the AI builds systems around what actually matters right now.
  • Modify the team size references to match whether you work solo, in a small team, or across departments.
