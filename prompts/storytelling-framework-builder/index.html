Storytelling Framework Builder

Build narratives using proven story frameworks like Hero's Journey, Three-Act Structure, and Before-After-Bridge for any context.

By Arshad Hossain

How to Use

Copy & paste the prompt below into your preferred LLM. Unless a specific AI model is mentioned, you can use whichever you prefer.

The Prompt

You are a storytelling expert who applies narrative frameworks to business, marketing, presentations, and creative writing. Help me build a compelling story.

My story:
- Context: [BUSINESS PRESENTATION / MARKETING CAMPAIGN / BRAND STORY / BLOG POST / SPEECH / BOOK / OTHER]
- Subject: [WHAT OR WHO IS THE STORY ABOUT?]
- Audience: [WHO WILL HEAR/READ THIS STORY?]
- Goal: [PERSUADE / INSPIRE / TEACH / ENTERTAIN / SELL]
- Key message: [WHAT SHOULD PEOPLE REMEMBER?]
- Raw material: [FACTS, EVENTS, DATA, OR EXPERIENCES TO WORK WITH]
- Length: [30 SECONDS / 2 MINUTES / 5 MINUTES / FULL ARTICLE / CHAPTER]

Deliver:
1. **Framework Selection:** Recommend the best story framework for your context:
   - Hero's Journey (transformation narrative)
   - Three-Act Structure (setup, confrontation, resolution)
   - Before-After-Bridge (marketing/sales)
   - Pixar Framework ("Once upon a time... Every day... One day... Because of that...")
   - The Mountain (building tension to a climax)
   With reasoning for why this framework fits
2. **Story Outline:** Complete outline mapped to the chosen framework with beats, turning points, and emotional arc
3. **The Draft:** Full story written out with:
   - A hook that creates immediate tension or curiosity
   - Specific sensory details (show, don't tell)
   - A moment of conflict or challenge
   - A resolution that delivers the key message
   - An ending that resonates
4. **Character Development:** Who the audience should root for and why (even in business stories)
5. **Emotional Arc Map:** The emotional journey from beginning to end, marked with highs, lows, and the turning point
6. **Adaptation Guide:** How to shorten for social media, expand for a keynote, or restructure for a different medium

Stories are the oldest technology for changing minds. Use them deliberately.

Why "Storytelling Framework Builder" Works

"Storytelling Framework Builder" is built on a principle most AI users overlook: models perform dramatically better when given structured enumeration and reasoning elicitation rather than open-ended questions. Expect publication-ready content that matches your target tone, audience, and word count requirements. The constraints in this prompt prevent the model from falling back on vague, unhelpful responses.

Pro Tips for Using "Storytelling Framework Builder"

These writing tips will help you get stronger results when using "Storytelling Framework Builder" and similar prompts in this category.

  • Request the AI to vary sentence length and structure - monotonous rhythm puts readers to sleep, while varied pacing maintains engagement.
  • Always specify your target audience and their reading level - writing for CEOs requires different language than writing for college students.
  • Ask the AI to start with a hook that creates curiosity or tension. Strong openings determine whether readers continue or bounce.

When to Use "Storytelling Framework Builder"

"Storytelling Framework Builder" is particularly useful in these situations. If any of these scenarios sound familiar, this prompt will save you significant time.

  • Your article needs a compelling introduction that hooks readers in the first two sentences.
  • You want to transform a dense technical document into an accessible piece that non-experts can understand and enjoy.
  • Your draft is complete but feels flat, and you need editing guidance that sharpens the argument and tightens the prose.

What You Will Get from "Storytelling Framework Builder"

When you use "Storytelling Framework Builder" with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, here is what to expect in the AI output.

  • Structured outlines with thesis statements, supporting arguments, transitions, and conclusion frameworks.
  • Audience-adapted content that adjusts vocabulary, tone, and complexity to match reader expectations.
  • Style-matched content that captures a specific voice, whether executive, conversational, or academic.

How to Customize "Storytelling Framework Builder"

Adapt "Storytelling Framework Builder" to your specific situation by modifying these key areas. The more context you add, the better the results.

  • Swap the generic topic with your specific angle or unique perspective on the subject.
  • Adjust the word count target to match your publication's requirements or the format's standard length.
  • Include your thesis or main argument so the AI builds supporting points around your actual position.
