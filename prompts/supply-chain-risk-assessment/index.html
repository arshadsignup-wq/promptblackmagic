Supply Chain Risk Assessment

Identify vulnerabilities in your supply chain and build contingency plans that prevent disruptions from derailing your business.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Paste into any LLM. Map out your supply chain details. Use the assessment to build resilience before problems arise.

The Prompt

You are a supply chain risk management consultant who has helped companies navigate disruptions from natural disasters, trade wars, pandemics, and supplier failures, saving clients millions in potential losses.

[PRODUCT/SERVICE]: What you produce or sell
[KEY SUPPLIERS]: Your critical suppliers (anonymized names are fine)
[SUPPLY CHAIN GEOGRAPHY]: Countries/regions involved
[SINGLE SOURCE DEPENDENCIES]: Any suppliers with no backup
[INVENTORY STRATEGY]: Just-in-time / Buffer stock / Mix
[ANNUAL COGS]: Cost of goods sold
[BIGGEST PAST DISRUPTION]: Worst supply issue you have faced

Conduct a comprehensive supply chain risk assessment:

**1. Supply Chain Mapping**
- Tier 1, 2, and 3 supplier identification
- Geographic concentration analysis
- Single point of failure identification
- Lead time analysis for each critical component
- Transportation route vulnerabilities

**2. Risk Identification Matrix**
- Natural disaster risks by geography
- Geopolitical and trade policy risks
- Financial health risks (supplier bankruptcy)
- Quality and compliance risks
- Cybersecurity and technology risks
- Capacity constraint risks
- Rate each: likelihood (1-5) x impact (1-5) = risk score

**3. Vulnerability Assessment**
- Critical vs. non-critical supply inputs
- Substitutability analysis for each input
- Lead time sensitivity analysis
- Cost impact modeling for key disruption scenarios
- Revenue impact of 1-week, 1-month, 3-month disruption

**4. Mitigation Strategies**
- Dual/multi-sourcing plan for critical inputs
- Strategic inventory buffer recommendations
- Supplier diversification roadmap
- Nearshoring or reshoring evaluation
- Contractual protections and SLAs

**5. Contingency Playbooks**
- Scenario 1: Primary supplier goes offline
- Scenario 2: Shipping route disruption
- Scenario 3: Raw material shortage
- Scenario 4: Quality failure in critical component
- For each: immediate actions, 48-hour plan, 30-day plan

**6. Monitoring Dashboard**
- Key risk indicators to track weekly
- Early warning signals
- Supplier scorecards
- Review cadence and escalation procedures

Pro Tips for Business

  • Ask the AI to structure its output as a step-by-step action plan rather than general advice - it forces concrete, implementable suggestions.
  • Include your budget constraints or resource limitations in the prompt so the AI recommends realistic solutions.
  • Request SWOT analysis format when evaluating opportunities - it ensures the AI considers both advantages and risks.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You want to identify revenue leaks in your business model and need a structured diagnostic framework.
  • You are negotiating a partnership deal and need talking points that position your company as the stronger collaborator.
  • You are writing a business plan for investors and need a structured framework that covers market analysis and financial projections.

Expected Results

  • SWOT analysis or competitive framework that maps strengths and threats to strategic decisions.
  • Financial projections or budget breakdowns with realistic assumptions and scenario modeling.
  • Standard operating procedures with step-by-step instructions that new team members can follow immediately.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Swap generic competitor names with your real top three competitors for actionable competitive analysis.
  • Modify the budget parameters to reflect your actual financial constraints so strategies are implementable.
  • Add your specific growth stage (startup, scaling, mature) to get stage-appropriate strategic guidance.
