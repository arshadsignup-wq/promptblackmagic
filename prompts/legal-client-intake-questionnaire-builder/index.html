Legal Client Intake Questionnaire Builder

Create comprehensive client intake forms for law firms that capture essential case information, conflict checks, and engagement terms for any practice area.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Customize this prompt for your practice area and jurisdiction. Use the output to build digital intake forms or printable questionnaires.

The Prompt

You are a law practice management consultant designing client intake systems.

Practice area: [FAMILY LAW / PERSONAL INJURY / BUSINESS / ESTATE PLANNING / CRIMINAL DEFENSE / IMMIGRATION / EMPLOYMENT]
Firm type: [SOLO / SMALL FIRM / MIDSIZE / CORPORATE]
Intake method: [ONLINE FORM / IN-PERSON / PHONE SCREENING / HYBRID]

Create a comprehensive client intake questionnaire:

1. **Contact information:** All necessary personal and contact details
2. **Case-specific questions:** 10-15 questions tailored to the practice area that capture the essential facts of the potential matter
3. **Timeline questions:** Key dates, deadlines, and statutes of limitation considerations
4. **Prior representation:** Questions about previous attorneys, pending cases, and related matters
5. **Conflict check fields:** Information needed to run conflict checks (opposing parties, related businesses, family members)
6. **Document checklist:** List of documents the client should bring or upload for the initial consultation
7. **Financial information:** Questions relevant to fee discussion (ability to pay, insurance coverage, contingency eligibility)
8. **Goals and expectations:** Open-ended questions about what the client hopes to achieve
9. **Referral source:** How they found the firm (for marketing tracking)
10. **Disclaimers:** Required disclaimers about attorney-client privilege, no guarantee of representation, and data handling

Format the questionnaire so it can be easily converted to a digital form (clear field types: text, dropdown, date, checkbox, file upload). Include conditional logic notes (e.g., if answer is X, show additional questions).

Pro Tips for Legal

  • Request that the AI distinguish between established law and emerging or unsettled areas where the legal landscape is still evolving.
  • Always include a disclaimer that AI-generated legal content is not legal advice and must be reviewed by a licensed attorney.
  • Specify the jurisdiction (federal, state, country) since laws vary dramatically and generic legal information can be misleading.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You need to research a legal issue and want a structured summary of applicable statutes, case law, and practical implications.
  • You received a contract and need to identify key risks, missing clauses, and negotiation priorities before signing.
  • You are a law student briefing cases for class and need consistent, well-structured case briefs with holdings and reasoning.

Expected Results

  • Structured legal research summaries organized by statute, case law, and practical implications with IRAC format.
  • Contract review analyses with risk ratings, missing clause identification, and suggested negotiation language.
  • Case briefs with procedural history, holdings, reasoning, and significance analysis in consistent format.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Adjust the audience level from attorney to client depending on who will read the output.
  • Include the opposing party details and relevant dates for case-specific document drafts.
  • Modify the citation format to match your court or firm requirements (Bluebook, ALWD, local rules).
