Patient Education Material Generator

Create clear, accessible patient education materials that explain medical conditions, treatments, and care instructions at an appropriate reading level.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Copy and paste this prompt into your preferred LLM. Replace bracketed placeholders with the specific medical topic and patient context. Always have a qualified healthcare professional review the output before distributing to patients.

The Prompt

You are a health communication specialist who creates patient education materials that are medically accurate and easy to understand.

Topic: [MEDICAL CONDITION, PROCEDURE, OR TREATMENT]
Patient audience: [AGE GROUP, LITERACY LEVEL, LANGUAGE CONSIDERATIONS]
Format: [HANDOUT / DISCHARGE INSTRUCTIONS / FAQ SHEET / CARE PLAN]

Create a patient education document that includes:

1. **Plain language overview:** Explain the condition/treatment in simple terms (6th-8th grade reading level). Avoid medical jargon or define it immediately when used.
2. **What to expect:** Timeline of what happens before, during, and after (if procedure-related)
3. **Self-care instructions:** Numbered, specific steps the patient can follow at home
4. **Warning signs:** Clear red-flag symptoms that require immediate medical attention, formatted as a prominent checklist
5. **Medications:** How to take prescribed medications, common side effects to watch for, and drug interactions to avoid
6. **Lifestyle modifications:** Diet, activity, and daily routine changes explained with practical examples
7. **Common questions:** 5-7 FAQs patients typically ask about this topic, with concise answers
8. **Follow-up:** When to schedule the next appointment and what to bring

Formatting rules:
- Use bullet points and numbered lists for scannability
- Bold key action items
- Include a "Call your doctor if..." section prominently
- Keep total length under 2 pages when printed

IMPORTANT: This content should be reviewed by a qualified healthcare professional before use. AI-generated medical information may contain errors.

Pro Tips for Healthcare

  • Ask the AI to cite evidence levels (RCT, meta-analysis, expert consensus) when generating clinical recommendations.
  • Specify whether the output is for patient-facing communication or provider-to-provider documentation - the language differs significantly.
  • Always include disclaimers that AI-generated healthcare content must be reviewed by qualified professionals before clinical use.

When to Use This Prompt

  • Your clinic is standardizing SOAP note documentation and you need consistent templates that meet billing and compliance requirements.
  • You are preparing a medical literature review for grand rounds and need a structured synthesis of recent evidence on a clinical topic.
  • Your team is building clinical training scenarios for residents or nursing staff and needs realistic patient cases with assessment rubrics.

Expected Results

  • Telehealth workflow documents covering patient preparation, provider checklists, and documentation templates.
  • Patient education materials written at the specified reading level with medical terms defined in plain language.
  • Structured clinical documentation (SOAP notes, care plans) following standard medical documentation conventions.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Include your facility type (hospital, clinic, telehealth) so workflows match your practice setting.
  • Modify the compliance framework references to match your specific regulatory requirements (HIPAA, Joint Commission, state-specific).
  • Add your EHR system name so documentation templates align with your charting platform.
