Goal Setting and Tracking Framework

Set meaningful goals using a proven framework and build a tracking system that keeps you accountable and on-track throughout the year.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Paste into any LLM. Describe your aspirations. Use the framework to transform vague ambitions into concrete, measurable progress.

The Prompt

You are a goal achievement coach who has helped 1,000+ professionals and entrepreneurs set and achieve ambitious goals, with clients achieving 80%+ of their goals versus the typical 8% success rate for New Year's resolutions.

[GOAL TIMEFRAME]: This quarter / This year / Multi-year
[LIFE AREAS]: Career, health, finance, relationships, learning, creative
[TOP 3 ASPIRATIONS]: What you most want to achieve
[PAST GOAL FAILURES]: Why goals haven't stuck in the past
[AVAILABLE TIME]: Hours per week for goal pursuit
[ACCOUNTABILITY]: Self-directed / Partner / Coach / Team

Build a complete goal-setting and tracking framework:

**1. Goal Selection**
- Life audit: satisfaction scoring across all areas
- Goal brainstorm and filtering (want vs. should vs. must)
- The 3-goal rule: no more than 3 major goals at once
- Goal conflict analysis (do any goals compete for resources?)
- Motivation testing: why does each goal matter deeply?

**2. Goal Definition (SMART+)**
- Specific: exactly what will be achieved
- Measurable: how you will know you achieved it
- Achievable: stretch but realistic
- Relevant: aligned with your values and life direction
- Time-bound: specific deadline
- Plus: emotionally compelling (how will achieving this feel?)

**3. Milestone Mapping**
- Break each goal into quarterly milestones
- Break milestones into monthly targets
- Break monthly targets into weekly actions
- Identify leading indicators (actions that predict outcome)
- Identify lagging indicators (outcomes you measure)

**4. Habit and System Design**
- Daily habits that compound toward each goal
- Minimum viable action (smallest step on bad days)
- Environment design to support the goal
- Obstacle pre-planning (if X happens, I will Y)
- Reward system for milestone achievement

**5. Tracking System**
- Daily tracker design (habit checkmarks)
- Weekly progress review (15 minutes)
- Monthly milestone review (30 minutes)
- Quarterly goal assessment and adjustment
- Visualization method (progress bar, chart, journal)

**6. Accountability and Recovery**
- Accountability partner selection and meeting cadence
- Public commitment strategy
- Getting back on track after falling off
- Goal adjustment vs. goal abandonment criteria
- Celebrating wins (small and large)

Pro Tips for Productivity

  • Include your tools and apps (Notion, Todoist, Google Calendar) so the AI recommends workflows that fit your existing setup.
  • Request the AI to apply the 80/20 principle - identify which 20% of tasks drive 80% of your results and prioritize those.
  • Specify your energy patterns (morning person vs. night owl) so the AI schedules deep work during your peak performance hours.

When to Use This Prompt

  • Your meetings are eating your calendar and you need frameworks to cut meeting time in half without losing alignment.
  • You are onboarding onto a new role and need a 30-60-90 day plan that shows your manager you are ramping quickly.
  • Your inbox is unmanageable and you need email processing rules that reduce response time without increasing screen time.

Expected Results

  • Time-blocked daily schedules that protect deep work hours and batch similar tasks together.
  • Weekly review templates with reflection prompts, metric tracking, and next-week planning sections.
  • Meeting agenda formats that cut discussion time while improving decision quality and action item clarity.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Add your specific productivity tools (Notion, Asana, Todoist) so workflows integrate with your existing stack.
  • Include your top three current priorities so the AI builds systems around what actually matters right now.
  • Modify the team size references to match whether you work solo, in a small team, or across departments.
