Comic Book Panel Layout Prompt

Describe a comic scene and get AI prompts for dynamic panel layouts with character acting, speech bubble placement, and sequential storytelling.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Use with Midjourney or Stable Diffusion comic models. Generate panels individually and compose in Photoshop or Clip Studio Paint.

The Prompt

You are a comic book artist who has drawn for Marvel, DC, and bestselling independent graphic novels.

Genre: [SUPERHERO/MANGA/INDIE/HORROR/SCI-FI/SLICE OF LIFE]
Scene description: [WHAT HAPPENS IN THIS PAGE]
Characters present: [LIST WITH BRIEF VISUAL DESCRIPTIONS]
Mood: [ACTION-PACKED/EMOTIONAL/SUSPENSEFUL/COMEDIC/DRAMATIC]
Page format: [AMERICAN COMIC 6.625x10.25/MANGA B5/GRAPHIC NOVEL 6x9]

Create a complete comic page prompt set:

**1. Full Page Layout Design**
- Panel arrangement (how many panels, their sizes and positions)
- Reading flow (left-to-right or right-to-left for manga)
- Panel that gets the most real estate (key moment)
- Gutter widths and bleed areas
- Speech bubble placement zones

**2. Establishing Panel (Panel 1)**
- Wide shot setting the scene
- Location, time of day, atmosphere
- Characters positioned in environment
- Color palette establishing mood
- Camera angle choice and reasoning

**3. Action/Dialogue Panels (Panels 2-4)**
- Medium shots for dialogue exchanges
- Character acting (expressions, body language, gestures)
- Shot/reverse shot for conversations
- Action lines and speed effects for movement
- Background simplification in dialogue panels

**4. Impact Panel (Largest Panel)**
- The page's most dramatic moment
- Dynamic pose, dramatic angle (worm's eye, bird's eye)
- Full background detail
- Sound effects lettering style
- Emotional peak of the page

**5. Transition / Closing Panel**
- Sets up the next page
- Cliffhanger or emotional beat
- Camera pulling back or pushing in
- Tonal shift from the impact panel
- Page-turn motivation

**6. Color Script**
- Overall page color palette
- How color shifts across panels to guide emotion
- Lighting consistency within the scene
- Spot color for emphasis (red blood, blue energy, etc.)
- Day/night color temperature

For each panel prompt: include camera angle (close-up, medium, wide, extreme), character expressions, background detail level, action lines, and emotional tone. Write as individual prompts that can be generated separately but compose into a cohesive page.

Pro Tips for Image Generation

  • Include negative prompts to exclude unwanted elements like blurry backgrounds, extra fingers, or distorted text in your generated images.
  • Reference specific art styles, lighting setups, and color palettes rather than vague descriptors like "beautiful" or "professional" for more consistent results.
  • Specify the aspect ratio and intended use (Instagram post, website hero, print) so the composition works for your platform.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You are designing marketing materials and need eye-catching visuals that match a specific brand aesthetic.
  • You need to visualize a concept, mockup, or prototype before investing in professional production.
  • You are exploring different visual styles for a rebrand and want to rapidly test multiple creative directions.

Expected Results

  • Complete negative prompt suggestions to avoid common AI image artifacts and unwanted elements.
  • Platform-specific aspect ratios and composition guidelines for each intended use case.
  • Color palette recommendations with hex codes that complement the subject and intended brand aesthetic.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Modify the camera and lens specs to match the look you want - wider for environmental, tighter for product detail.
  • Replace the product description with your actual product name, dimensions, materials, and key visual features.
  • Swap the style preference for your brand aesthetic - minimal, luxury, playful, bold, or organic.
