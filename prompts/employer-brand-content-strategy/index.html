Employer Brand Content Strategy

Get a complete employer branding playbook with content pillars, social media strategy, and employee advocacy program design.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Paste into any LLM with your company details. Use the output to attract better candidates through content.

The Prompt

You are an employer branding strategist who has helped companies become 'best places to work' and cut recruiting costs by 50%.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Team size: [SIZE]
Hiring needs: [ROLES YOU'RE RECRUITING FOR]
Current employer brand perception: [STRONG/WEAK/UNKNOWN]
Glassdoor rating: [IF KNOWN]

Build a complete employer brand strategy:

**1. Employee Value Proposition (EVP)**
- Define your EVP in one sentence
- 4-5 EVP pillars with proof points
- How it differentiates from competitors' EVPs
- Messaging framework for different audiences (engineers vs marketers vs execs)

**2. Content Pillars (6 themes)**
- Day-in-the-life content
- Growth and learning stories
- Culture and team moments
- Impact and mission content
- Behind-the-scenes / how we build
- Employee spotlights

**3. Channel Strategy**
- LinkedIn company page: posting cadence, content types, engagement tactics
- Instagram/TikTok: visual culture content, formats that work
- Glassdoor: response templates, review encouragement
- Careers page: must-have sections, copy guidelines
- Job description brand integration

**4. Employee Advocacy Program**
- How to recruit internal advocates
- Content sharing guidelines and templates
- Incentive structure
- Training for employees on personal branding
- Compliance guardrails

**5. Content Calendar**
- 30-day content calendar with specific post ideas
- Content batch production workflow
- User-generated content collection system
- Event-based content opportunities

**6. Measurement**
- Application quality metrics
- Source-of-hire tracking
- Employer brand awareness surveys
- Social media engagement benchmarks
- Cost-per-hire impact

Provide specific copy examples and templates throughout.

Pro Tips for HR & People Management

  • Specify whether the output is for the employee or the manager perspective since the framing differs significantly.
  • Specify the company size, industry, and work arrangement (remote/hybrid/on-site) since HR practices vary dramatically by context.
  • Ask the AI to include specific behavioral examples and scoring rubrics rather than generic competency descriptions.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You are conducting interviews and want to eliminate bias with structured scorecards and behavioral questions.
  • You want to build an employer brand that attracts top talent without relying solely on recruiters.
  • You are hiring for a critical role and your job postings are getting low-quality applicants or very few responses.

Expected Results

  • Ready-to-use templates with specific questions, scoring criteria, and evaluation frameworks.
  • Day-by-day or week-by-week schedules with activities, milestones, and check-in agendas.
  • Communication scripts for difficult conversations including feedback delivery and termination discussions.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Replace the role title with your actual position including level, department, and reporting structure.
  • Swap the company size and industry references with your specific organizational context.
  • Adjust the competency frameworks to match your company values and the skills most critical for the role.
