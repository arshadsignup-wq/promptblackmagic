Competitor Analysis Deep Dive

Name your competitors and your product. Get a strategic analysis with positioning gaps, feature comparisons, and opportunities to differentiate.

By Arshad Hossain

How to Use

List your competitors and what you know about them. Use the analysis to refine your positioning and strategy.

The Prompt

Conduct a thorough competitive analysis for my business.

My company/product: [WHAT YOU SELL AND WHO YOU SELL TO]
My top competitors:
1. [COMPETITOR 1 NAME AND WHAT THEY DO]
2. [COMPETITOR 2 NAME AND WHAT THEY DO]
3. [COMPETITOR 3 NAME AND WHAT THEY DO]

My current pricing: [YOUR PRICE POINT]
My main differentiator: [WHAT YOU THINK MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]

Analyze:

1. Feature comparison matrix:
   - Table comparing key features across all competitors and me
   - For each feature: who does it best and why
   - Gaps: features they all have that I lack (urgent to address)
   - Opportunities: features nobody offers yet that I could own

2. Positioning analysis:
   - How each competitor positions themselves (their messaging, tagline, target audience)
   - Where there is white space in the market nobody is claiming
   - A 2x2 positioning map with suggested axes relevant to my industry

3. Pricing analysis:
   - Compare pricing models (freemium, subscription, one-time, usage-based)
   - Where am I on the price spectrum and is that the right position?
   - Pricing strategy recommendations

4. Strengths to attack: Where each competitor is weakest (based on reviews, common complaints)

5. Threats to defend: Where I am most vulnerable

6. Strategic recommendations: Top 5 actions I should take based on this analysis, prioritized by impact and effort

7. Messaging angles: 3 marketing messages that highlight where I win vs. each competitor

Why "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive" Works

What makes "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive" worth using over writing your own prompt is the engineering behind it. The depth requirements and analytical framing and reasoning elicitation built into this well-structured prompt took multiple iterations to refine. You can expect a clear strategic framework with specific action steps tailored to your business context - the kind of result that normally requires several rounds of prompt refinement.

Pro Tips for Using "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive"

These business tips will help you get stronger results when using "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive" and similar prompts in this category.

  • Ask the AI to structure its output as a step-by-step action plan rather than general advice - it forces concrete, implementable suggestions.
  • Request SWOT analysis format when evaluating opportunities - it ensures the AI considers both advantages and risks.
  • When prompting for business strategy, provide context about your industry, company size, and current challenges for actionable advice.

When to Use "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive"

"Competitor Analysis Deep Dive" is particularly useful in these situations. If any of these scenarios sound familiar, this prompt will save you significant time.

  • You are writing a business plan for investors and need a structured framework that covers market analysis and financial projections.
  • You are entering a new market and need competitive intelligence organized into actionable insights.
  • You are preparing a pitch deck and need concise value propositions that communicate your business model in seconds.

What You Will Get from "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive"

When you use "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive" with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, here is what to expect in the AI output.

  • Executive-ready summaries that distill complex analysis into clear recommendations with supporting evidence.
  • A structured action plan with specific milestones, timelines, and resource requirements.
  • Financial projections or budget breakdowns with realistic assumptions and scenario modeling.

How to Customize "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive"

Adapt "Competitor Analysis Deep Dive" to your specific situation by modifying these key areas. The more context you add, the better the results.

  • Swap generic competitor names with your real top three competitors for actionable competitive analysis.
  • Add your specific growth stage (startup, scaling, mature) to get stage-appropriate strategic guidance.
  • Replace the industry placeholder with your specific sector so recommendations reflect real market dynamics.
