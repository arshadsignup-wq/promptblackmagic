Editing and Proofreading Assistant

Get detailed feedback on your writing with line-level edits, structural suggestions, and style improvements.

By Arshad Hossain

How to Use

Copy & paste the prompt below into your preferred LLM. Unless a specific AI model is mentioned, you can use whichever you prefer.

The Prompt

You are a senior editor at a top publishing house. Review my writing with the precision of a copy editor and the vision of a developmental editor.

My writing:
- Text to edit: [PASTE YOUR WRITING HERE]
- Type: [BLOG POST / ESSAY / EMAIL / REPORT / STORY / ACADEMIC PAPER / MARKETING COPY / OTHER]
- Target audience: [WHO WILL READ THIS?]
- Goal: [INFORM / PERSUADE / ENTERTAIN / INSTRUCT / SELL]
- Tone I'm going for: [DESCRIBE DESIRED TONE]
- Specific concerns: [ANYTHING YOU'RE UNSURE ABOUT?]
- Edit level wanted: [LIGHT PROOFREAD / MODERATE EDIT / HEAVY RESTRUCTURE]

Deliver a three-layer edit:

1. **Structural Edit:**
   - Is the overall structure logical? Does it flow?
   - Are there sections that should be reordered, expanded, or cut?
   - Is the opening strong enough? Does the ending land?
   - Are transitions smooth between paragraphs?

2. **Line Edit:**
   - Flag awkward sentences and rewrite them
   - Cut unnecessary words (aim for 20% shorter)
   - Replace weak verbs with strong ones
   - Fix passive voice where active is better
   - Eliminate clichés and suggest fresh alternatives
   - Improve rhythm and readability

3. **Copy Edit:**
   - Grammar, spelling, and punctuation corrections
   - Consistency checks (tense, style, terminology)
   - Fact-check any claims that seem questionable

Provide the fully edited version AND a summary of the 5 most important changes with explanations of why each makes the writing stronger.

Be honest. I want to improve, not feel good.

Why "Editing and Proofreading Assistant" Works

"Editing and Proofreading Assistant" is built on a principle most AI users overlook: models perform dramatically better when given role assignment and sequential task breakdown rather than open-ended questions. Your output will be publication-ready content that matches your target tone, audience, and word count requirements - the difference between useful AI assistance and a response you immediately delete.

Pro Tips for Using "Editing and Proofreading Assistant"

These writing tips will help you get stronger results when using "Editing and Proofreading Assistant" and similar prompts in this category.

  • Always specify your target audience and their reading level - writing for CEOs requires different language than writing for college students.
  • Ask the AI to start with a hook that creates curiosity or tension. Strong openings determine whether readers continue or bounce.
  • Include examples of writing styles you admire so the AI can emulate specific qualities like wit, clarity, or emotional depth.

When to Use "Editing and Proofreading Assistant"

"Editing and Proofreading Assistant" is particularly useful in these situations. If any of these scenarios sound familiar, this prompt will save you significant time.

  • Your article needs a compelling introduction that hooks readers in the first two sentences.
  • You want to transform a dense technical document into an accessible piece that non-experts can understand and enjoy.
  • Your draft is complete but feels flat, and you need editing guidance that sharpens the argument and tightens the prose.

What You Will Get from "Editing and Proofreading Assistant"

When you use "Editing and Proofreading Assistant" with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, here is what to expect in the AI output.

  • Audience-adapted content that adjusts vocabulary, tone, and complexity to match reader expectations.
  • Style-matched content that captures a specific voice, whether executive, conversational, or academic.
  • Edited drafts with tracked changes showing tightened prose, stronger verbs, and clearer arguments.

How to Customize "Editing and Proofreading Assistant"

Adapt "Editing and Proofreading Assistant" to your specific situation by modifying these key areas. The more context you add, the better the results.

  • Swap the generic topic with your specific angle or unique perspective on the subject.
  • Adjust the word count target to match your publication's requirements or the format's standard length.
  • Include your thesis or main argument so the AI builds supporting points around your actual position.
