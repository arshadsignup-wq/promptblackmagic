Tattoo Design Reference Generator

Describe your tattoo concept and get AI prompts for multiple tattoo styles - from traditional to geometric - with placement and sizing considerations.

By The Prompt Black Magic Team

How to Use

Use with Midjourney (--style raw works best for clean linework) or Stable Diffusion. Share results with your tattoo artist as reference.

The Prompt

You are a tattoo designer with 15 years of experience across all major tattoo styles who also creates reference art for custom tattoo commissions.

Subject/concept: [WHAT THE TATTOO DEPICTS]
Placement: [ARM/FOREARM/BACK/CHEST/LEG/SHOULDER/WRIST/ANKLE/RIBS]
Size: [SMALL 2-3 INCH/MEDIUM 4-6 INCH/LARGE 8+ INCH/FULL SLEEVE]
Meaning or story: [PERSONAL SIGNIFICANCE]

Create 6 tattoo style variations:

**1. Traditional / Old School (American Traditional)**
- Bold black outlines, limited color palette (red, green, yellow, blue)
- Classic iconography and composition rules
- Flat color fills, minimal shading
- Banner text options
- Flash sheet presentation

**2. Fine Line / Single Needle**
- Delicate, thin lines with no bold outlines
- Minimalist approach to the subject
- Subtle shading through dot work or light stippling
- Clean, elegant negative space
- Works best at smaller sizes

**3. Japanese (Irezumi) Style**
- Traditional Japanese composition rules
- Wind bars, water, clouds, and background fill
- Bold outlines with gradient shading (bokashi)
- Color palette following traditional conventions
- Flow and movement with the body

**4. Geometric / Sacred Geometry**
- Subject integrated with geometric patterns
- Precise symmetry and mathematical relationships
- Dotwork shading and mandala elements
- Balance between organic subject and geometric structure
- Clean, technical precision

**5. Blackwork / Woodcut**
- Heavy black ink, no color
- Cross-hatching and parallel line shading
- Inspired by medieval woodcut prints
- Strong contrast and dramatic shadows
- Works well at medium to large sizes

**6. Watercolor Style**
- Color splashes extending beyond the subject outline
- Drips, splatters, and color bleeds
- Light or minimal linework as skeleton
- Vibrant, flowing color transitions
- Artistic and painterly feel

For each style: write as a complete AI generation prompt, specify black and white or color, include placement-specific composition notes (how the design wraps around the body part), and provide negative prompts to avoid photorealistic skin/body elements.

Pro Tips for Image Generation

  • Reference specific art styles, lighting setups, and color palettes rather than vague descriptors like "beautiful" or "professional" for more consistent results.
  • Specify the aspect ratio and intended use (Instagram post, website hero, print) so the composition works for your platform.
  • Describe the mood with 5-8 specific adjectives rather than generic terms - "warm golden hour glow with soft bokeh" beats "nice lighting" every time.

When to Use This Prompt

  • You need product photography for your e-commerce store but cannot afford a professional photographer or studio rental.
  • You are building a social media presence and need consistent, on-brand visual content across multiple platforms.
  • You want to create unique art or illustrations for a project without hiring a graphic designer.

Expected Results

  • Platform-specific aspect ratios and composition guidelines for each intended use case.
  • Color palette recommendations with hex codes that complement the subject and intended brand aesthetic.
  • Detailed, copy-paste ready prompts for AI image generators with camera settings, lighting specs, and style keywords.

How to Customize This Prompt

  • Change the platform specification to match where the image will be used (Instagram square, website banner, print ad).
  • Adjust the lighting and mood descriptors to match your brand photography guidelines.
  • Add your brand colors as hex codes so the AI can incorporate them into backgrounds and styling.
