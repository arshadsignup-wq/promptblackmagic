AI Prompts for Podcast Production: Scripts, Show Notes, and Promotion

Producing a podcast involves far more writing than most people realize. Before you hit record, you need episode outlines, guest research briefs, and talking points. After recording, you need show notes, transcription summaries, social media clips, email newsletters, and blog post adaptations. For a weekly show, that's 20-30 pieces of written content per month on top of the actual recording and editing. After building and testing hundreds of content creation prompts, we've found that AI handles podcast production writing better than almost any other content type because podcasts follow predictable structures that AI can replicate and customize efficiently.

This guide introduces the SOUND Method, a five-stage framework for using AI across every phase of podcast production, from initial concept to cross-platform distribution.

Why Podcasters Need AI More Than Other Content Creators

Bloggers write one piece of content and publish it. Podcasters create one piece of content (the episode) and then need to repurpose it into a dozen different formats for different platforms. Show notes for the website. Clips for Instagram and TikTok. Thread summaries for LinkedIn. Newsletter recaps for email subscribers. Audiogram captions. Guest thank-you messages. Each format has different length requirements, tone expectations, and platform conventions.

According to Edison Research's Infinite Dial report, there are over 4 million podcasts but only a fraction publish consistently beyond the first 10 episodes. The production burden - not a lack of ideas - is the primary reason podcasts die. AI doesn't replace the host's voice and expertise. It eliminates the production bottleneck that prevents good podcasters from sustaining their shows.

The SOUND Method: Five Stages of AI-Powered Podcast Production

S - Structure Episodes

Every great podcast episode follows a structure, whether the host acknowledges it or not. Interview shows typically follow: hook, guest intro, origin story, deep dive on topic, actionable takeaways, close. Solo shows often use: provocative opening, context setting, main argument with supporting points, counterarguments, conclusion with call to action.

AI excels at generating episode structures because these patterns are well-established. The key is prompting with enough specificity about your show's format, audience, and goals.

Our Article Outline Builder prompt demonstrates the same structural approach applied to written content. Adapt its framework for audio by adding timing estimates, transition cues, and listener engagement prompts (questions to pose, moments to pause for emphasis).

For each episode, prompt AI to generate:

  • Cold open hook: A 15-30 second attention grabber that makes the listener commit to the full episode. The best hooks present a surprising fact, a contrarian opinion, or a relatable problem
  • Segment breakdown: 3-5 segments with estimated times, key points per segment, and transition sentences that maintain narrative flow
  • Question bank (for interviews): 15-20 questions organized from broad to specific, with follow-up probes for each. Always prepare more questions than you need
  • Closing framework: A consistent outro structure that includes a call to action, teaser for the next episode, and a memorable sign-off line

O - Outline Talking Points

Episode structure gives you the skeleton. Talking points give you the muscle. This is where AI research capabilities become invaluable, especially for interview-based shows where you need to demonstrate knowledge of your guest's work without reading from a script.

For guest episodes, prompt AI to research:

  • Guest background brief: Career timeline, notable achievements, recent projects, published content, public opinions, and potential controversy areas to avoid or address
  • Unique angle identification: What has this guest NOT been asked in other podcast appearances? What's the fresh angle your audience hasn't heard? AI can analyze transcripts from the guest's previous appearances to identify unexplored territory
  • Audience relevance mapping: How does this guest's expertise connect to your specific audience's challenges? Map their knowledge to your listeners' pain points

Our Content Ideation Consultant prompt is designed for exactly this kind of creative research. Use it to generate episode concepts that connect trending topics to your podcast's niche, ensuring every episode feels both timely and relevant to your core audience.

For solo episodes, prompt AI to build talking point briefs that include statistics, case studies, counterarguments, and real-world examples. The goal isn't to script every word. It's to walk into the recording booth with enough prepared material that you can speak confidently and naturally without rambling.

U - Unpack Insights

After recording, you have raw audio full of insights that need to be extracted and organized. This stage transforms your episode from a single audio file into a content goldmine.

Start by feeding your episode transcript (or detailed notes) to AI and prompt it to:

  • Extract key quotes: Pull the 5-10 most compelling, shareable statements from the episode. These become social media posts, audiogram captions, and newsletter highlights
  • Identify main takeaways: Summarize the 3-5 actionable insights a listener should walk away with. These become your show notes structure
  • Flag surprising moments: Identify unexpected turns, contrarian opinions, or emotional peaks in the conversation. These are your best clip candidates
  • Generate timestamps: Create a timestamped topic index so listeners can jump to sections that interest them

The unpack stage is where AI saves the most time. Manually reviewing a 60-minute episode for these elements takes 2-3 hours. AI does it in minutes, and you spend your time editing and refining rather than listening through the entire episode again.

N - Notes for SEO

Show notes are the most underutilized SEO asset in podcasting. Most podcasters write a 2-3 sentence summary and call it done. That's a missed opportunity. Show notes are web pages that Google indexes, and well-written show notes can drive organic traffic to your podcast for months or years after the episode airs.

According to Google's podcast indexing documentation, Google surfaces podcast episodes in search results based on the text content associated with each episode. Thin show notes mean thin search visibility.

Prompt AI to generate show notes that include:

  • SEO-optimized title: Include your target keyword naturally. "How Sarah Chen Built a $5M SaaS Without Funding" outperforms "Episode 47 - Interview with Sarah Chen"
  • Episode summary: 150-300 words covering the main topics, written for someone deciding whether to listen. Include your primary and secondary keywords
  • Detailed topic breakdown: H2 and H3 headings for each major topic discussed, with 2-3 sentence summaries under each. This structure helps both SEO and reader scanning
  • Resource links: Every tool, book, website, or resource mentioned in the episode, hyperlinked and organized
  • Guest bio and links: Brief bio with links to the guest's website, social profiles, and any resources they mentioned
  • Transcript excerpt: A compelling 200-300 word excerpt from the episode that entices reading and provides additional indexable content

D - Distribute Across Channels

One episode should generate content for every platform you're active on. AI makes this repurposing systematic rather than ad hoc.

Our Viral Social Media God Prompt can be adapted for podcast promotion by feeding it your episode's key insights and asking for platform-specific content. But distribution goes beyond social media posting.

For each episode, prompt AI to create:

  • Twitter/X thread: 5-8 tweets summarizing key insights with a hook tweet and a CTA to listen
  • LinkedIn post: Professional framing of the episode's business takeaways (300-500 words)
  • Instagram carousel script: 8-10 slides distilling the episode into a visual story
  • Email newsletter: A subscriber-first recap that adds the host's personal take and exclusive context not in the episode
  • Blog post adaptation: A 600-800 word article derived from the episode's core topic, optimized for a different long-tail keyword than the show notes
  • Audiogram captions: 3-5 punchy 15-second quotes formatted for video captions with speaker attribution
  • YouTube description: SEO-optimized description with timestamps, relevant links, and subscribe CTAs

Building a Podcast Content Calendar with AI

Consistency is the strongest predictor of podcast success. Use AI to build a quarterly content calendar that balances topic variety with strategic themes.

Prompt AI with your podcast's niche, audience demographics, and business goals. Ask it to generate a 12-episode calendar (one quarter) that includes:

  • Episode topics: Varied enough to stay interesting, focused enough to serve your niche
  • Content mix: Balance between solo episodes, guest interviews, Q&A episodes, and special formats
  • Seasonal relevance: Tie episodes to industry events, seasonal trends, and your audience's planning cycles
  • Content arcs: Multi-episode series that encourage listeners to come back for part 2 or part 3

Measuring Podcast Growth

Track these metrics monthly:

  • Downloads per episode: Your core listenership metric
  • Show notes page views: Measures your SEO effectiveness
  • Social engagement rate: Which promotional content drives the most interaction
  • Listener completion rate: What percentage listen to the full episode vs. dropping off early
  • Review and rating velocity: How frequently new reviews appear

Start Producing Smarter Today

Pick your next episode and apply the SOUND Method end to end. Structure it with AI, outline your talking points, record as usual, then let AI unpack the insights, write the show notes, and generate distribution content. Most podcasters report saving 5-8 hours per episode on production tasks, time they reinvest into improving the actual content.
