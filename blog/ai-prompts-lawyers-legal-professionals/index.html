AI Prompts for Lawyers: Legal Research, Contract Review, and Client Communication

The legal profession's relationship with AI is evolving rapidly. Courts now address AI use in filings, bar associations are issuing guidance on ethical AI use, and law firms that ignore AI risk falling behind on efficiency. This guide provides practical prompts for legal workflows with clear guardrails about where AI helps and where human expertise remains essential.

The BRIEF Framework for Legal AI Prompts

Legal prompts require a specific framework we call BRIEF:

  • B - Boundaries: Define the jurisdiction, practice area, and scope of the legal question
  • R - Reliability check: Include instructions for the AI to flag uncertainty and distinguish binding authority from persuasive authority
  • I - Independent verification: Every prompt should remind users to verify citations using Westlaw, LexisNexis, or official court databases
  • E - Ethical compliance: Ensure prompts align with professional responsibility rules (competence, confidentiality, candor to tribunal)
  • F - Format for purpose: Structure output for its intended use (client memo, court filing, internal analysis)

Legal Research

Structured Research Summaries

AI excels at organizing legal research into structured frameworks. Our Legal Research Summary Generator produces IRAC-formatted analysis with statutory references, case law summaries, and practical implications. The prompt includes specific instructions for the AI to distinguish between binding and persuasive authority.

Critical limitation: AI models - including Claude and ChatGPT - can and do fabricate case citations. A 2023 federal court case famously sanctioned attorneys who submitted a brief with AI-generated citations to nonexistent cases. Every citation in AI research output must be independently verified. No exceptions.

Case Briefing

Law students and attorneys reviewing case law benefit from AI-assisted case briefing. The Case Brief Writer produces consistently structured briefs with procedural history, material facts, issues, holdings, and reasoning. When you paste the actual court opinion, the output quality improves dramatically compared to asking the AI to brief a case from memory.

Contract Review and Drafting

Contract Analysis Workflow

Contract review is one of AI's strongest legal applications because the source material is right there in the prompt. Our Contract Review Checklist Prompt analyzes pasted contracts for key terms, risk areas, missing clauses, and negotiation priorities.

The workflow: paste the contract text, specify which party you represent, and the AI produces a structured review. Because it is working from the actual document rather than its training data, hallucination risk is significantly lower than in research tasks. However, it can still miss jurisdiction-specific requirements or nuanced implications that experienced attorneys catch.

Plain Language Translation

Clients often receive legal documents they cannot understand. The Legal Document Plain Language Translator converts complex legal text into clear explanations at an 8th-grade reading level. This is valuable for client communication, informed consent processes, and consumer-facing disclosures.

Client Communication and Practice Management

Demand Letters

The Demand Letter Framework drafts professional demand letters with proper legal structure - factual recitation, legal basis, specific demands, and response deadlines. The AI handles formatting and tone while the attorney provides the facts and legal analysis.

Client Intake Systems

Efficient intake processes improve client experience and reduce administrative burden. Our Legal Client Intake Questionnaire Builder creates practice-area-specific intake forms with conflict check fields, document checklists, and conditional question logic.

Privacy Policies and Compliance

Digital businesses need legally sound privacy policies and terms of service. The Privacy Policy and Terms Generator creates comprehensive documents covering GDPR, CCPA, and industry-specific requirements. These always need attorney review before publication, but AI provides a strong starting framework that saves hours of drafting time.

Ethical Considerations for AI in Legal Practice

  • Competence (Rule 1.1): Using AI tools requires understanding their limitations. Submitting AI output without review may violate competence obligations.
  • Confidentiality (Rule 1.6): Do not input client confidential information into general-purpose AI tools unless the platform has appropriate data protection agreements.
  • Candor to tribunal (Rule 3.3): AI-generated citations must be verified before inclusion in any court filing.
  • Supervision (Rules 5.1, 5.3): Attorneys must supervise AI output just as they supervise work from associates or paralegals.
  • Billing transparency: Firms should develop clear policies on how AI-assisted work is billed to clients.

The American Bar Association and most state bars have issued or are developing guidance on AI use in legal practice. Stay current with your jurisdiction's requirements.
